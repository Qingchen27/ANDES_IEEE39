# ANDES_IEEE39
mutli-instability for power systems

It should be noted that the generated dataset is highly imbalanced across admissible label combinations, which reflects the intrinsic occurrence frequency of different post-fault mechanisms in the simulated transmission-grid scenarios rather than a purely artificial sampling bias. Stable samples constitute the largest class, while voltage- and frequency-related states are more common than pure angle-instability states. This is physically consistent with the studied systems, which retain a synchronous-machine backbone and only partially replace selected generation locations with converter-interfaced renewable injections: under such settings, post-fault responses more often manifest as insufficient voltage recovery or local bus-frequency excursions than as isolated loss of synchronism. Moreover, the adopted angle-instability criterion is stringent ($\delta_{\max}\ge 360^\circ$), so pure angle-instability states remain rare, whereas angle instability more often appears together with other modes. Therefore, the observed label imbalance should be understood as a physically induced data characteristic of CMII in renewable-integrated transmission grids.
